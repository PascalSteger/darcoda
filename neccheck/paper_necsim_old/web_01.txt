<h2>research interests</h2>
I'm intersted in galaxy formation. My main research area is on dark matter with methods of numerical astrophysics.

<h2>projects</h2>
For my Bachelor thesis, I used simulations of galaxy clusters to determine misalignment of angular momentum, shape and environment from dark matter and baryonic components of halos. A toolset dubbed A<small>MATEUR</small> was developed to analyze S<small>PH</small> snapshots.

I now investigate smaller scales for my Master thesis, working on dark matter in dwarf spheroidals. High resolution simulations with unprecendented non-equilibrium physics up to <i>z</i>=10 are used to determine the density profiles of stars, dark matter and gas. This allows to shed new light on the cusp-core problem. Comparison with a second simulation with the same initial conditions, but only dark matter, allows to determine the influence of baryons on the formation of cores.
